Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers to common questions about Sonorium.

Why don't I hear any sound?

The most common causes are:

  • Speaker compatibility: Your media player doesn't support HTTP audio streams
  • Network issue: The stream URL isn't accessible from your speaker (firewall, different subnet)
  • Volume: Channel volume or master gain is set to 0
  • Device muted: The speaker itself is muted or volume is down

Troubleshooting steps:

  1. Try playing to a different speaker to isolate the issue
  2. Check the Sonorium logs for errors
  3. Verify your stream URL setting matches your network
  4. Test the stream URL directly in a browser
My speakers aren't showing up in Sonorium

Sonorium discovers speakers from Home Assistant's media_player entities. Check the following:

  • Your speakers are added as media_player entities in Home Assistant
  • The speakers aren't disabled in Sonorium Settings
  • Try clicking "Refresh Speakers" in Settings
  • Verify the Home Assistant API connection is working

Speakers must support HTTP audio streaming. Most Chromecast, Sonos, and DLNA devices work well.

What audio formats are supported?

Sonorium supports these formats:

  • .mp3 - Most compatible, recommended
  • .wav - Uncompressed audio
  • .flac - Lossless compression
  • .ogg - Open format

Recommendations:

  • Use MP3 files at 128-320kbps for best compatibility
  • Longer tracks (1-10 minutes) work best for seamless looping
  • Tracks with natural fade-in/fade-out blend better
Where do I put my audio files?

Easiest: Use the App

Open Sonorium, go to Themes, and create themes directly in the interface. You can upload audio files via drag-and-drop.

Home Assistant (File System):

Place audio files in /media/sonorium/. Create a subfolder for each theme:

/media/sonorium/Thunder/distant_thunder.mp3
/media/sonorium/Thunder/rain_on_roof.mp3
/media/sonorium/Forest/birds.mp3

Windows App (File System):

Place audio files in the themes folder next to Sonorium.exe.

What does "Presence" do in the Track Mixer?

Presence controls how often a track appears in the mix (0-100%).

  • 100%: Track plays continuously
  • 50%: Track fades in and out, present about half the time
  • 20%: Track appears occasionally

This is perfect for occasional sounds like thunder claps, bird calls, or door creaksâ€”set presence to 20-40% so they appear naturally without looping constantly.

What's the difference between playback modes?
  • Auto: Automatically selects the best mode based on file length
  • Continuous: Loop continuously with seamless crossfade (best for background sounds)
  • Sparse: Play once, wait 30-300 seconds, repeat (best for short sounds)
  • Gapless: Loop without crossfade (for tracks designed to loop perfectly)
  • Presence: Fade in/out based on presence setting
Can I use Sonorium with Sonos/Google Home/Alexa?

Yes! Any speaker that appears as a media_player entity in Home Assistant and supports HTTP audio streaming will work:

  • Sonos: Full support via Home Assistant integration
  • Google Home/Chromecast: Works great with cast devices
  • Amazon Echo: Requires additional setup (check HA community)
  • DLNA/UPnP speakers: Most network speakers work
  • AirPlay: Works with AirPlay-compatible devices
Where can I get ambient audio files?

Several options for finding quality ambient audio:

  • Included themes: Sleigh Ride and Tavern come with Sonorium
  • Freesound.org: Large library of free sound effects
  • Ambient-Mixer.com: Downloadable ambient soundscapes
  • YouTube Audio Library: Free ambient tracks
  • MyNoise.net: High-quality ambient generators
  • Record your own: Nature recordings, rain on windows, etc.
Will the Windows/Mac app support network speakers?

Yes! Network streaming to external speakers is on the roadmap for standalone apps (Windows, Mac, Linux) and Docker.

Currently, the Windows app plays through your PC's audio output. Future versions will include:

  • Chromecast/Google Cast support
  • DLNA/UPnP streaming
  • AirPlay support
Is Sonorium really free?

Yes! Sonorium is completely free and open source under the MIT license.

It's a fork of the original Amniotic project by fmtr, with significant new features added.

The source code is available on GitHub. Contributions are welcome!
