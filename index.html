Getting Started

Up and running in minutes with your choice of installation.

1

Add the Repository

Click the button below or manually add the repository URL to your Home Assistant Add-on Store.

Or manually: Settings → Add-ons → ⋮ → Repositories → Add: https://github.com/synssins/sonorium

2

Install & Start

Find "Sonorium" in the Add-on Store, click Install, then Start. The addon will appear in your sidebar.

3

Add Your Audio

Create theme folders in /media/sonorium/ with your audio files. Two starter themes are included!

/media/sonorium/ ├── Thunder/ │ ├── distant_thunder.mp3 │ └── rain_on_roof.mp3 ├── Forest/ │ ├── birds.mp3 │ └── wind_leaves.mp3 └── Your Theme/ └── your_audio.mp3
4

Create a Channel

Open Sonorium from your sidebar, click "New Channel", select a theme and your speakers, then hit Play!

Extract & Run

Extract the zip file and run Sonorium.exe. No installation required—it's fully portable.

4

Start Mixing

Select a theme, adjust your mix, and enjoy ambient soundscapes through your PC speakers or connected audio device.

Everything You Need

Powerful features to create the perfect ambient atmosphere in every room.

Multi-Channel Audio

Run up to 10 independent audio channels simultaneously. Each channel can play a different theme to different speakers.

Theme-Based Mixing

Organize audio into themes that automatically blend together. Thunder, rain, wind—they all mix seamlessly in real-time.

Track Mixer

Fine-tune individual tracks with volume, presence, and playback controls. Create the exact atmosphere you want.

Presets

Save your favorite track configurations as presets. Switch between "morning focus" and "deep sleep" modes instantly.

Export & Import

Package themes as zip files to share with others. Import themes from the community with a single click.

Plugin System

Extend functionality with plugins. Import soundscapes from ambient-mixer.com or build your own integrations.

Broadcast Model

All speakers on a channel hear the exact same stream, like a radio station. Join mid-stream without restarting.

Live Speaker Management

Add or remove speakers from an active channel without stopping playback. Changes take effect immediately.

Smart Short Files

Short sounds like bird calls play once then wait naturally, rather than looping annoyingly. Fully configurable.

Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers to common questions about Sonorium.

Why don't I hear any sound?

The most common causes are:

  • Your media player doesn't support HTTP audio streams (check speaker compatibility)
  • The stream URL isn't accessible from your speaker (firewall or network issue)
  • Channel volume or master gain is set to 0
  • The speaker is muted or volume is down on the physical device

Try playing to a different speaker to isolate the issue.

My speakers aren't showing up in Sonorium

Sonorium discovers speakers from Home Assistant's media_player entities. Make sure:

  • Your speakers are added as media_player entities in Home Assistant
  • The speakers aren't disabled in Sonorium Settings
  • Try clicking "Refresh Speakers" in Settings

Speakers must support HTTP audio streaming (most Chromecast, Sonos, and DLNA devices do).

What audio formats are supported?

Sonorium supports the following formats:

  • .mp3 - Most compatible
  • .wav - Uncompressed audio
  • .flac - Lossless compression
  • .ogg - Open format

For best results, use MP3 files at 128-320kbps. Longer, seamlessly looping tracks work best for ambient soundscapes.

Where do I put my audio files?

Home Assistant: Place audio files in /media/sonorium/. Create a subfolder for each theme (e.g., /media/sonorium/Thunder/).

Windows App: Place audio files in the themes folder next to the Sonorium.exe. Create subfolders for each theme.

You can also upload files directly through the Sonorium web interface by going to Themes and using drag-and-drop.

What does "Presence" do in the Track Mixer?

Presence controls how often a track appears in the mix (0-100%). At 100%, the track plays continuously. At lower values, the track fades in and out naturally over time.

This is great for occasional sounds like thunder claps or bird calls—set presence to 20-40% so they appear periodically rather than constantly.

Can I use Sonorium with Sonos/Google Home/Alexa?

Yes! Any speaker that appears as a media_player entity in Home Assistant and supports HTTP audio streaming will work. This includes:

  • Sonos - Full support via Home Assistant integration
  • Google Home/Chromecast - Works great with cast devices
  • Amazon Echo - Requires additional setup (check HA docs)
  • DLNA/UPnP speakers - Most network speakers work
Where can I get ambient audio files?

Several options for finding quality ambient audio:

  • Ambient Mixer Plugin - Import soundscapes directly from ambient-mixer.com
  • Freesound.org - Large library of free sound effects
  • YouTube Audio Library - Free ambient tracks
  • Record your own - Nature recordings, rain on windows, etc.

Two themes (Sleigh Ride and Tavern) are included with Sonorium to get you started!

Is Sonorium really free?

Yes! Sonorium is completely free and open source. It's a fork of the original Amniotic project by fmtr, with significant new features added.

The source code is available on GitHub. Contributions are welcome!

Who It's For

Ambient soundscapes aren't just background noise—they're tools for wellbeing.

Built from Personal Need

Sonorium was created by someone who is AuDHD (autism + ADHD) and needs ambient sound to function. Background audio quiets the part of the brain that's really good at distracting everything else.

  • ADHD & Focus: White noise and nature sounds help filter distracting sounds and improve concentration
  • Misophonia: Ambient masking helps cover trigger sounds and reduce emotional responses
  • Sensory Processing: Controlled ambient environments provide predictable, soothing auditory input
  • Anxiety & Stress: Nature sounds activate the parasympathetic nervous system, promoting relaxation
  • Sleep: Consistent ambient sound masks disruptive noises for better rest
  • Work & Study: The "coffee shop effect"—moderate ambient noise boosts creative thinking
